A leading home furnishings retailer came to us with a familiar problem: a beautiful catalog of 7,000+ products and shoppers who couldn't find what they were looking for. Bounce rate sat at 65%. Cart abandonment was the highest it had been in two years.

## The problem

The site search worked. The filters worked. The product pages were gorgeous. But the customer journey was a maze — *"I want a sofa for a small apartment that's pet-friendly and ships in two weeks"* is not a query a faceted search can answer.

> Customers weren't bouncing because the site was broken. They were bouncing because there was no one to ask.

## The implementation

We connected Nexus² to:

- The product catalog (live inventory, materials, dimensions, ship-by dates)
- Existing brand voice and styling guidance
- The CMS articles and care guides
- The order and returns systems for logged-in shoppers

Two weeks from kickoff, Nexi was live on the storefront — chat on every page, voice on the call line, and an in-cart helper for shoppers who got stuck.

## The result

After 90 days:

- **Bounce rate**: 65% → 41%
- **Cart abandonment**: down 35%
- **Average order value**: up 18% (Nexi cross-sells naturally)
- **Support tickets**: down 28% — most "where is my order" questions never reach a human

## Why it worked

Nexi didn't replace the storefront. It made the storefront answerable. Every customer got the equivalent of a knowledgeable salesperson who'd read every product spec — at 3am on a Tuesday.
